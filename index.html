Introduction

What is COVID-19?

  • Covid-19 is a novel coronavirus: a strain of coronavirus not previously identified.
  • The virus Covid-19 is not the same as the coronaviruses that cause mild illnesses, such as the common cold


    • What is the big deal?

  • New strain of coronavirus. Since Covid-19 is new, there is no widespread immunity in the population, which makes communities more susceptible to the virus.
  • Fast spreading. The virus is fast spreading and is thought to be transferred mainly person to person via droplets that result from coughing or sneezing.


    • Understanding Exponential Growth

  • The fast, initial spread of Covid-19 roughly fits an exponential curve. This means that the total number of confirmed multiplies every few days. For example, looking at the growth of new cases in the US, we can see that the total number of confirmed Covid-19 cases grows roughly 4 times in size every 5 days.


  • For comparison, we can look at the number of deaths per day due to pneumonia and influenza during the 2017-2018 season. In the lower graph, growth is notably much slower. It took around 6 months before the number of pneumonia and influenza related deaths per day increased by a factor of 4.


  • In less than 2 weeks, the number of Covid-19 related deaths per day grew by the same factor. This rapid spread makes Covid-19 dangerous to the public as it can quickly overwhelm hospital resources in a short amount of time.

    Should you wait?

    Timely implementation matter?

    Social distancing, also called “physical distancing,” means keeping space between yourself and other people outside of your home. Left chart includes new confirmed cases in 15 heavily affected countries and the average days before its national government issued a shelter-in-place order (average about 50 days).

    Delayed Effect

    Newly confirmed cases in most countries only began to flatten 2-3 weeks after social distancing measurements are implemented. For instance, in the case of Spain, it took 15 days before new cases reached its growth peak and started to stabilize.

    Reactive or Preactive

    Countries such as Iran, Brazil or Italy with faster social distancing policies response (below average waited time) normally associate with a faster-growing and steeper exponential curve than countries such as Japan or Singapore. This may be an indication that social distancing policies are more of a reactive measurement than a proactive preventative decision.

    Conclusion

    Covid-19 has brought unprecedented challenges for individuals, families, and countries across the world. The exponential growth of this novel coronavirus has left people and policy makers with uncertainty about how long this pandemic will last and how many more lives will be impacted. Without available vaccines and effective treatments, orders on social distancing are enacted by different countries’ national and local governments to lower interpersonal contact rate. For many of the heavily affected countries, social distancing has achieved a pronounced effect in containing the viral spread, usually within 2 weeks after implementation. Although it doesn’t come without costs, policy makers and each individual should consider practicing social distancing in a timely manner for the safety of the country, the vulnerable ones nearby or yourself.
